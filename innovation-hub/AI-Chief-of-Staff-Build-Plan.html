The reframe

Stop building seven products. Build four layers, once.

Each system below is a feature someone wants to build in parallel. That's the trap — seven half-built tracks and no working loop. Collapse them into a stack where each layer does one job and hands off to the next.

The trap · seven tracks at once
AI Calendar Email ERP Workflow engine Mobile app Voice system

Seven moving parts, no single working path. Nothing demos until everything works — so nothing demos.

The four layers

Most of the logic lives in the middle. The UI stays thin.

Layers 1 and 4 are plumbing and polish. The product's intelligence — and its defensibility — sits in layers 2 and 3.

L1

System connectors

ingestion only

Just connect to the systems of record over their APIs and pull events, approvals, tasks, emails and meeting notes into one shared shape. No reasoning here — that's the point. Get clean, normalised data in.

Google CalendarMicrosoft 365GmailOdoomonday.comSlackWhatsApp
L2

Executive knowledge graph

the secret sauce

Instead of leaving data trapped in separate systems, link every entity — meeting, approval, person, project, task, risk, decision, customer. Once they're connected, the AI can reason across them instead of just listing them. Without this layer, you're just another dashboard.

MeetingApprovalPersonProjectTaskRiskDecisionCustomer
L3

Intelligence engine

mission control

Every few minutes: collect events, identify risks and decisions, rank attention, generate the briefing. Most of this starts as plain rules — IF approval > $100K AND project blocked → create attention item — and only later leans on the model. You don't need AI for everything.

CollectIdentify riskIdentify decisionsRank attentionGenerate briefing

Experience layer

already prototyped

This is the part that already exists: the executive briefing, mission control, approval flow, team risk and meeting actions. Because the logic lives in L2 and L3, the UI stays thin — which is exactly why the demo could be built first.

Recommended stack

Boring, proven pieces — chosen to fit the team that's building it.

Given a background strong in Zoho, enterprise systems and automation, the stack leans on what that team already reasons about well. Nothing exotic; every choice is replaceable later.

.NET 10
or Node.js · LTS, supported to 2028

Right call — no need for a graph DB at this stage.

What to build first

Three months, three loops — each one provable on its own.

Don't build features; build closing loops. Each month delivers one end-to-end path you can put in front of an executive, and the third is where the defensibility lives.

Month 1
Approval intelligence
Odoo→AI rec→approve→write-back

Prove the loop. One approval fetched, checked against policy, recommended, decided, and written back to the ERP.

Month 2
Meeting intelligence
transcript→minutes→actions→monday/Odoo

Turn a transcript into minutes, extract action items with owners and dates, and push them to the task tracker.

Month 3
Team risk intelligence your moat
assigned tasks+daily updates→mismatch→risk score

Compare what people say they're doing against what they're assigned. Detecting that gap is the hardest — and most defensible — thing here.

The hard part

The biggest challenge isn't AI, UI, or integrations. It's attention scoring.

One number decides what rises to the top of the executive's day. Get the weights right and the whole product feels intelligent; get them wrong and it's noise. Over time this score becomes the moat.
